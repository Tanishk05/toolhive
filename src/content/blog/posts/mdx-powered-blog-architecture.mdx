## Start with a content contract

Your blog system gets easier to maintain when each post follows the same contract. Frontmatter should describe the page, and the body should focus on the narrative.

```ts
type PostFrontmatter = {
  title: string;
  excerpt: string;
  date: string;
  category: string;
  tags: string[];
  author: string;
};
```

## Keep rendering separate from data loading

Static generation works best when the loader does one job and the renderer does another. That means metadata, reading time, and related posts are derived before the page renders.

### Why that matters

- You can pre-render archive pages.
- You can build RSS and sitemap entries from the same source.
- You avoid duplicate logic across route handlers.

## Use content as a product surface

Once your blog is registry-driven, it stops being a special case. You can sort it, filter it, test it, and extend it without rewriting the page structure.

## A simple rule

If a blog page needs a custom exception, make it data, not code.
